IMPARTIAL ANALYSIS BY COUNTY COUNSEL
CITY OF CALIPATRIA MEASURE D



Article 3.7, Chapter 4 of Part 1 of Division 2 of Title 5 of the California
Government Code (Section 53720 et seq.) was added by Initiative Measure
approved by the people, November 4, 1986. Sections 53720 et seq. allow
local governments to impose a general tax for the purpose of providing
revenue for general governmental purposes, including police and fire services, street lighting purposes, and park and street maintenance.

This tax shall be in addition to the annual tax allowed under Article XIII A
of the California Constitution. The general tax imposed shall be a tax upon
each parcel of property and the tax shall not be measured by the value of the
property. The maximum general tax imposed by Ordinance No. 249 shall be
as follows:

The maximum general tax on all parcels described on the assessment
roll by the Imperial County Tax Assessor with improvements as of March 1
of each year shall be One Hundred Fifty Dollars ($150.00).

The maximum general tax on all parcels described on the assessment
roll by the Imperial County Tax Assessor without improvements as of
March 1 of each year shall be One Hundred Twenty-Five Dollars ($125.00).
The ordinance, the authorization to levy the general tax, will become
effective only if approved by a majority of the voters voting on this issue. The
authorization for this general tax shall expire on June 30.1992.

A “Yes” vote is a vote to approve the ordinance imposing a general tax for
the purpose of providing revenue for general governmental purposes.

A “No” vote is a vote against approving the ordinance imposing a general
tax for the purpose of providing revenue for general governmental purposes.
