Impartial Analysis from City Attorney

Based on a resolution adopted by a two-thirds vote of the City Council, an ordinance is proposed to impose a retail transactions and use tax of one-half of one percent (1/2%) in order to provide additional funds for general governmental purposes of the City, such as police, fire, emergency medical, library, recreation, local street maintenance, and other governmental purposes as determined from time to time by the City Council. The proposed tax will increase the local sales tax rate in Richmond from the current 8.25% to 8.75%. Local sales taxes contain certain exemptions and exclusions, such as fixed price contracts and leases of tangible personal property which will not be affected by the ordinance.
